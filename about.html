About

A program built shoreline.

Long Wharf Junior Roller Derby is the first competitive junior derby program on the Connecticut coast. We are volunteer-run, skater-led, and building something that did not exist here before.

Mission

Build the most respected junior roller derby program in the Northeast. Develop skaters who can make the A-team at any league they go to. Produce coaches, Team USA candidates, and All-Stars. Earn a reputation as dangerous.

We are not a recreational program. We are a developmental program with the explicit goal of producing skaters who can compete at the highest levels of junior derby and transition into elite adult derby. Every decision we make is calibrated to that mission.

What roller derby is

Roller derby is a full-contact sport played on quad roller skates on a flat oval track. Two teams of five skaters compete in two-minute jams. Each team's jammer scores points by lapping opposing blockers; the blockers work together to stop the other team's jammer while clearing a path for their own.

It is fast. It is physical. It is deeply strategic. It rewards athletes who think as hard as they hit, and it has long been one of the most welcoming sports in the world — explicitly inclusive of all genders, identities, body types, and backgrounds.

Who can join

  • Anyone ages 12 to 17, regardless of prior derby experience
  • You bring quad roller skates, a certified helmet, knee pads, elbow pads, wrist guards, and a mouthguard
  • You bring the willingness to fall down, get back up, and do it again
  • We teach everything else

Our teams

Long Wharf JRD operates a tiered team structure. Skaters progress through tiers based on formal skill assessments, not on age, popularity, or pressure. Every skater starts where they start and advances when they are ready.

Travel team · Level 3+

Amity Mariners

The chartered, full-contact competitive team. Mariners represent Long Wharf in inter-league play. The face of the program.

B-team · Level 2

Savin Rock Whalers

Skaters with the fundamentals, learning to apply them in derby contexts. Modified-contact scrimmages, controlled intensity, preparation for full contact.

Development · Level 0–1

Long Island Sound Periscopes

Skaters new to derby. Foundation work, skating fundamentals, building toward visibility. Submerged for now. Coming up fast.

The codes we live by

Every Long Wharf skater signs the MARINERS code of conduct. Every parent or guardian signs the AMITY code of conduct. These are not formalities. They are the standard we hold ourselves and our community to. Truncated below; the full versions live in the registration packet.

MARINERS - for skaters

M
Make Space for Everyone

Derby is for everyone. You are responsible for making this team a place where every skater feels like they belong.

A
Answer the Whistle

Coaching decisions and officiating calls belong to the people in those roles. Trust the process and keep skating.

R
Rise Every Time You Fall

Derby will put you on the floor. So will life. What matters is what you do next.

I
Integrity On and Off Skates

How you conduct yourself when no one is watching is the same as how you conduct yourself on the track.

N
No One Skates Alone

Derby is a team sport even when you are the jammer. Your teammates work for you. You work for them.

E
Earn Your Level

Skill progression keeps everyone safe. You advance when you are ready, not when you are impatient.

R
Respect the Contact

Full contact is part of this sport. It is not a license to be reckless. You can play hard and still care about your opponent.

S
Show Up

To practice, to your team, and to yourself. Commitment means more than attendance. Be present.

AMITY — for parents and guardians

A
Amplify, Don't Undermine

Your voice from the sideline shapes your child's experience more than almost anything. Your child hears all of it.

M
Make It Their Game

This sport belongs to your child. Decisions on the track belong to the coaches and the skaters.

I
Include Everyone in the Stands

The sideline is a community. How you treat the people around you sets the tone for what kind of league this is.

T
Trust the Process

Skill levels exist for safety. Playing time and advancement are coaching decisions, made on what coaches observe at practice.

Y
You Are the Example

Your child watches how you react to every call, every hit, every loss. Be the fan you want them to grow up to be.
